Tribute For APJ Abdul Kalam

Early Life

☛Dr.A.P.J Abdul Kalam was Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a Tamil Muslim family. His father, Jainulabdeen Marakayar, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. His mother was Ashiamma.
☛The family business, involving trading groceries and ferrying pilgrims between Rameswaram and the mainland, collapsed with the opening of the Pamban Bridge in 1914. This led to the loss of their fortune and properties, leaving them poverty-stricken.
☛Being the youngest of five siblings, Kalam had to contribute to the family's income from a young age. He sold newspapers as a boy to support his family financially.
☛Despite having average grades in school, Kalam was a bright and hardworking student with a strong passion for learning. He showed a particular interest in Mathematics.
☛After completing his education at Schwartz Higher Secondary School, he graduated in physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, affiliated with the University of Madras. His dream of becoming a fighter pilot was narrowly missed, but he later pursued aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology in Madras (now Chennai).

Personal Life

☛Kalam was the youngest of five siblings, and he had a close and affectionate relationship with his elder brothers and sister. He maintained strong bonds with his extended family and regularly supported them financially.
☛Kalam was known for his integrity and simplicity. He lived a modest life, devoid of materialistic possessions, and had minimal personal belongings.
☛Religion and spirituality were significant aspects of Kalam's life. As a practicing Sunni Muslim, he performed daily namaz (prayers) and observed fasting during Ramadan.
☛Kalam's upbringing, with his father being an imam and his close association with individuals from various faiths, instilled in him a deep respect for different religions. He believed in interfaith dialogue and cooperation, promoting unity among diverse traditions.
☛Kalam's spiritual quest led him to seek guidance from Pramukh Swami, the Hindu guru of the BAPS Swaminarayan Sampradaya. He found inspiration and transformation through their interactions,
☛The interactions & inspirations leading him to write his final book:Transcendence: My Spiritual Experiences with Pramukh Swamiji which reflected his spiritual experiences and views.

Last Days

☛On July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture on Creating a Livable Planet Earth at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. Dr. Kalam experienced discomfort and collapsed just five minutes into his speech. He was rushed to the nearby Bethany Hospital in critical condition, where he was confirmed dead of a sudden cardiac arrest at 7:45 p.m. IST.
☛ Dr. Kalam's passing was met with shock and grief across the nation and the world. His body was airlifted to New Delhi, where it was received by dignitaries and laid in state at his residence. People from all walks of life paid their respects, including political leaders, ministers, and citizens.
☛On July 29, 2015, Dr. Kalam's body was flown from Delhi to Madurai and then taken to Rameswaram, his hometown. The body was displayed in an open area in front of the local bus station, allowing the public to pay their final respects until 8 p.m
☛On July 30, 2015, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was laid to rest at Rameswaram's Pei Karumbu Ground with full state honors. Over 350,000 people attended the last rites, including the Prime Minister, the governor of Tamil Nadu, and the chief ministers of neighboring states.
☛Dr. Kalam's passing marked the end of an era for India, losing a visionary leader, a renowned scientist, and a beloved figure who had inspired millions. His legacy as the Missile Man of India and his contributions to the nation continued to be remembered and revered by generations to come.

Awards & Honours

☛Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam received a total of 7 honorary doctorates from 40 universities, recognizing his outstanding contributions to science and technology.
☛The Government of India honored him with the prestigious,Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his exceptional work with ISRO and DRDO, as well as his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.
☛In 1997, Dr. Kalam was bestowed with India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, in recognition of his immense contribution to scientific research and the modernization of defense technology in the country.
☛In 2013, he received the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society in recognition of his excellent management and leadership of space-related projects.
